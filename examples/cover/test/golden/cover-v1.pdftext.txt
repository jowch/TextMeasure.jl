Nothing on the page knows its neighbors in advance. The title does not reserve room for the column, and the column does
not reserve room for the quote. Each element asks the same measuring tape how wide it is, and the finished layout simply
falls out of the answers it gets back.
That is the whole trick, and it is a quiet one. There is no template to fill, no grid to fight, and no pixel pushed by hand at
the end to make a seam disappear. The seams never appear at all, because the seams were computed shut long before any
ink was laid on the page.
So the page is a proof rather than a paste up. What you are reading flowed around the art automatically, top to bottom and
left to right, with the same engine that will lay out the next cover and the one after that. Measure once, then compose
