The exhibit measures once and lays
out many times. A single prepared
paragraph is packed around an
inset-exclusion chord function that
subtracts the illustration, the drop cap,
and the pull quote from every scanline
band before a single glyph is ever
drawn. Correctness is asserted on the
computed layout, never recovered
from the rendered file.
Because the packer fills every interval
a band offers, the column wraps on
both sides of a centered illustration at
once. Text streams down the left
margin and the right margin in the
very same lines, threading past the
skyline without a hand-placed break
anywhere in sight.
Widen the inset, narrow it, or slide it
toward either edge of the column, and
the surrounding prose simply
rebalances around the new shape. The
measurements do not care where the
picture lands. They report how much
room each line has, and the greedy
packer takes it from there.
